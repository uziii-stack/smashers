THE WILLOW

The 'Salix alba Caerulea' Willow Tree thrives in damp soil and will typically grow around bodies of water. Their extensive root systems and large capacity for water retention mean that they can help manage floodplains by stabilising surrounding soils and preserving nutrient rich topsoils. Their growth rate is considerable and, when managed, allows a Salix Alba to reach maturity in 15-20 years.

One intriguing quality of the Salix Alba is its tremendous ability to both capture and store carbon. Its rate of high energy growth is driven through the assimilation of carbon dioxide through photosynthesis, which makes the tree highly effective in the global effort to reduce high concentrations of CO2 in the atmosphere.
